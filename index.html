Celebrating Hispanic Heritage Month

Hispanic Heritage Month began as Hispanic Heritage Week in 1968. Established by President Lyndon B.Johnson. Hispanic Heritage Month highlights the contributions of more than 60 million Hispanic Americans in areas such as politics, arts, science, and beyond.

SO EXCITING: Native American Literary Festival

The Fine Print celebrates Native American Heritage through our November Literary festival, where people of Native American heritage are invited to share their stories, peoms, and more. To publish, you must be of Native American heritage, and/or have written a submission to Native American heritage history. Submissions are due by November 22nd, 11:59 PM EST.

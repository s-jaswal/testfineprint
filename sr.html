Scientific Writing




Qualities that are commonly shown in high-quality Scientific Writing


Clarity

When submitting an article, the writing should be free of ambiguity or unnecessary jargon. A clear and uncomplicated explanation of the research and data results.

Cohesion

When writing is cohesive, it means that the ideas the writer is trying to convey are organized and interconnected. There are many ways to achieve this, such as transitional phrases like “additionally” or “despite”. Proper grammar also is a crucial way to communicate the transition in your ideas.

Conciseness

Somewhat of an extension to clarity, but overall just being straight-to-the-point without adding filler to your writing ensures that you won't lose your reader while you're explaining your data/research.

Keep In Mind

Scientific writing is quite different from creative writing, so keeping these three qualities in mind while writing will definitely be useful in improving your writing skills. :)




Time management
Responsibilities of Scientific Writing editors

Scientific Writing editors have a bunch of responsibilities such as going through staff applications, AI/grammar-checking article submissions, attending meetings and writing articles for the websites monthly articles. Being able to handle all of these responsibilities in a timely manner is one of the many wonderful qualities that the editors have! Time management is paramount to success in The Fine Print's Scientific Writing Team because it ensures that everything comes out on time and with good quality.

Scientific Writing Skills
The Skills Needed

Since editors have to read through scientific article/ research paper submissions, they need the skills to identify the components of scientific writing. This is so they can give relevant feedback to the submitter and thus improve their scientific writing skills.

Positive Attitude
Just don't be a poopyhead ✨

Being kind to everyone, and overall not being rude to others is the most important quality of being a part of the scientific writing team. Being genuinely kind to the people that surround you can open countless opportunities, and we want to reflect that at The Fine Print’s Scientific Writing Team!

Each month, our amazing Scientific Editors release two new short articles for the public to read. One article will be about a scientific topic (ex: genetic engineering), and another article will be about minority researchers in correspondence to the month (ex: Hispanic STEM researchers for Hispanic Heritage Month). Each month celebrates something unique (Black History Month, Women's History Month, etc) and as a non-profit that is dedicated to amplifying the voices of minorities, we will celebrate that to our fullest extent!
